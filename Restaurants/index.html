Commander's Palace

The Commander's Palace is one of the most renowned restaurants in New Orleans. It has been part of the city for more than a hundred years now ! It is a real New Orleans landmart, nestled in the middle of the tree-lined Garden District, and is known for its convivial atmosphere, as well as the outstanding quality of its food. Multiple renowned chefs have worked here, making this place the world-class restaurant it is today.

Café du Monde

If there's one iconic bar in New Orleans, it is surely the Café du Monde. If you've seen a report on New Orleans, there's a 75% chance you've seen one of the 11 Café du Monde in New Orleans in this report. This coffee shop serves mainly French specialties, including beignets, which are a variety of French doughnuts shaped as a square and covered with powdered sugar, or "café au lait", which is coffee mixed with hot milk. Multiple coffees are served, and each of them can be chosen black or "au lait". The Café du Monde also serves fresh orange juice, as well as chocolate and white milk. It is open every day, 24 hours a day, closing only on Christmas.

Tipitina's

Tipitina's started as a little neighborhood juke joint in 1877, established by a group of young music fans to provide a place for Professor Longhair (one of the most revered rhythm and blues musician in the legacy of New Orleans music) to perform in his final years. It was named after the most enigmatic recording of Professor Longhair, Tipitina. Throughout the years and decades, more and more renowned New Orleans artists came here to perform. Some still perform today : Dr John, the Neville Brothers, Galactic... In short, Tipitina's is the perfect place if you want to hear the music of New Orleans while sipping a cocktail.

Pat O'Brien's Bar

Even though the official bar opened in 1933, after the end of the Prohibition, Pat O'Brien already ran the bar as a speakeasy in New Orleans. It the place where the "duelling pianos" concept thrived. This concept showcases two pianists "duelling" for the attention and the tips of the audience. This concept thrived in Pat O'Brien's Bar because here, the public could request the musics that would be played. Naturally, this show became famous and appreciated really quickly, and word of it spreaded through the journals, boosting the bar's famousness. Piano duels are really a thing we recommend you to see before leaving New Orleans, so don't miss the opportunity to go at Pat O'Brien's Bar !

The Spotted Cat Music Club

Recognized as an international destination for Jazz music, the Spotted Cat Music Club is one of the quintessential music places of New Orleans. It appeared in multiple movies, commercials or other media, and has been praised for its unique ambiance and the outstanding quality of its music. It even appears on the cover of the official New Orleans Tourist book ! This music club also has the peculiarity of not accepting any reservations. So, if you wanna get a place in this club, you'll probably need to come 10-15 minutes in advance, depending on the hour.

Napoleon's House

200 years ago, Nicholas Girod (mayor of New Orleans of 1812-1815) offered his residence to Napoleon in 1921, as a refuge for the exiled emperor. Napoleon never made it to New Orleans, but the name stuck to the building. It became one of the most famous restaurants in America, and is placed on the National Registry of Historic Places since 1970. It changed ownership only a few times, being held by the Impastato family from 1914 to 2015, and then being bought by Ralph Brennan. It's a place suspended in time, in which you can hear Beethoven's Eroiqua, which he composed for Napoleon, while sipping a Pimm's cup and eating Muffuletta.

Brennan's

Constructed in 1795, the building that hosts this famous restaurant once housed the Louisiana State Bank, served as a private residence, and was home to the eccentric chess master Paul Morphy. It was sold to the Brennan family in 1984, and was restored in 2013. It is now one of the most famous restaurants in New Orleans, due to its quality and the name it has. The Brennan family is known for being a family of real food and wine connoisseurs, so a restaurant possessing their name is surely a good one ! The restaurant also has the peculiarity of hosting a family of ten turtles in its backyard.
